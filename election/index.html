🏛️ Overview

How India Elects

India follows a parliamentary system with elections at national, state, and local levels — all overseen by the Election Commission of India (ECI).

01
🏛️

Lok Sabha (Lower House)

543 members elected directly by citizens from constituencies across India. The party or coalition with majority forms the central government. Elections held every 5 years.

02
🏢

Rajya Sabha (Upper House)

245 members — 233 elected by state legislatures using Single Transferable Vote, and 12 nominated by the President. Members serve 6-year terms, with one-third retiring every 2 years.

03
📋

State Assemblies (Vidhan Sabha)

Each state has its own legislative assembly. Members (MLAs) are directly elected by citizens. The majority party forms the state government headed by the Chief Minister.

04
🏘️

Local Body Elections

Panchayats (rural) and Municipal Corporations (urban) elections. These three-tier local governance bodies handle grassroots administration. Conducted by State Election Commissions.

05
⚖️

Election Commission of India

An autonomous constitutional body that supervises elections. Headed by the Chief Election Commissioner and two Election Commissioners. Ensures free, fair, and impartial elections.

06
🗳️

First-Past-The-Post (FPTP)

India uses the FPTP system — the candidate with the most votes in a constituency wins. No minimum vote share required. Simple, direct, and easy for voters to understand.

📅 Election Timeline

From Announcement to Results

A general election unfolds across several weeks in carefully planned phases.

Phase 1 · Pre-Election

📢 Election Announcement

The ECI announces election dates, schedule, and the Model Code of Conduct (MCC) comes into effect immediately.

  • The MCC is a set of guidelines for political parties and candidates during elections
  • Government cannot announce new policies or projects after MCC is enforced
  • ECI sets up election machinery, appoints observers, and review electoral rolls
  • Typically announced 6-8 weeks before the first polling date
Phase 2 · Nominations

📝 Filing of Nominations

Candidates file their nomination papers along with an affidavit declaring assets, liabilities, criminal cases, and educational qualifications.

  • A security deposit of ₹25,000 (General) or ₹12,500 (SC/ST) is required
  • Candidates must be proposed by registered voters of the constituency
  • Scrutiny of nominations happens within a few days
  • Last date for withdrawal of candidature is also specified
Phase 3 · Campaign

📣 Election Campaigning

Parties and candidates campaign through rallies, door-to-door outreach, media, and social media. Campaigning must stop 48 hours before polling.

  • Campaign spending is capped — ₹95 lakh for Lok Sabha, ₹40 lakh for Assembly
  • No appeals to caste, religion, or communal feelings allowed
  • "Silence period" — 48 hours before voting, no campaigning is permitted
  • ECI monitors media coverage for fairness
Phase 4 · Polling Day

🗳️ Voting Day

Voters cast their votes using Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) with VVPAT verification. Polling happens in multiple phases across the country.

  • Polling usually from 7 AM to 6 PM, varies by region
  • Voter must carry valid photo ID (EPIC, Aadhaar, Passport, etc.)
  • Indelible ink applied on left index finger to prevent duplicate voting
  • NOTA (None of the Above) option available since 2013
  • VVPAT slip shown for 7 seconds to verify vote
Phase 5 · Counting

📊 Counting & Results

Votes are counted electronically. Results are declared constituency by constituency. The party with majority is invited to form the government.

  • Counting typically begins at 8 AM on the designated date
  • Postal ballots are counted first, followed by EVM votes
  • VVPAT paper slips of 5 randomly selected polling stations are matched with EVM count
  • 272 seats needed for majority in Lok Sabha (out of 543)
  • Results usually available by evening on counting day
Phase 6 · Government Formation

🏛️ Oath & Government

The President invites the majority party/coalition leader to form government. The PM and Council of Ministers take oath of office.

  • If no single party wins majority, coalition negotiations begin
  • The new PM must prove majority on the floor of the House
  • Council of Ministers appointed — Cabinet, Ministers of State, Deputy Ministers
  • New Lok Sabha must convene within 30 days of results

🚶 Voter Journey

Your Step-by-Step Voting Guide

From registration to casting your vote — here's everything you need to know.

📋 Voter Registration

Every Indian citizen aged 18+ must register to vote. Here's how:

  • Online: Visit voters.eci.gov.in and fill Form 6
  • Offline: Submit Form 6 at your nearest ERO (Electoral Registration Officer) office
  • Documents: Age proof, address proof, and passport-size photo
  • EPIC: After verification, you receive your Voter ID card (EPIC)
  • NRI Voting: NRIs can register using Form 6A with passport details
  • Voter Helpline: Call 1950 or use the Voter Helpline App

📑 Prepare for Voting Day

Be ready before election day with these steps:

  • Check Roll: Verify your name on the electoral roll at voters.eci.gov.in
  • Find Booth: Locate your polling station using the Voter Helpline App
  • Carry ID: EPIC, Aadhaar, Passport, DL, or any of the 12 accepted IDs
  • Know Candidates: Study candidate affidavits on the ECI website
  • Holiday: Polling day is a paid holiday — your employer must allow you to vote
  • Accessibility: Wheelchairs, braille ballots, and pick-up services available for PwD voters

🗳️ Casting Your Vote

Here's exactly what happens at the polling station:

  • Step 1: Queue up at your assigned polling booth
  • Step 2: Show your ID to the polling officer for verification
  • Step 3: Indelible ink is applied on your left index finger
  • Step 4: Enter the voting compartment with the EVM
  • Step 5: Press the button next to your chosen candidate's name & symbol
  • Step 6: Verify on the VVPAT slip (shown for 7 seconds)
  • Step 7: Exit — your vote is recorded and sealed

✅ After You Vote

Your democratic duty doesn't end at the booth:

  • Ink Mark: The indelible ink stays for about 2-4 weeks — wear it proudly!
  • Track Results: Watch live counting on results.eci.gov.in
  • Stay Informed: Follow ECI announcements for any re-polling updates
  • Grievances: Report any malpractice via cVIGIL app or call 1950
  • Engage: Participate in local governance, attend gram sabhas, and stay civically active
  • Next Election: Update your voter details if you move to a new address
🔢 Step-by-Step

Election Day Process

What happens behind the scenes to ensure a secure and transparent election.

01
🔒

EVM Preparation

EVMs are prepared, tested, and sealed in the presence of candidate representatives. Each machine is randomized to a polling station.

02
🏫

Booth Setup

Polling stations are set up in schools, community halls, etc. CCTV cameras, webcasting, and micro-observers ensure transparency.

03
👮

Security Deployment

Central Armed Police Forces (CAPF) are deployed. Sensitive and critical booths get extra security. Flying squads patrol for violations.

04
🗳️

Polling & Recording

Mock poll conducted before voting begins. Every vote is electronically recorded in the EVM and a paper trail is generated by VVPAT.

05
📦

Sealed & Stored

After polling ends, EVMs are sealed with unique serial numbers and stored in strong rooms under 24/7 CCTV surveillance and armed guard.

06
🏆

Counting & Declaration

On counting day, EVMs are opened in the presence of agents. Round-by-round results are tallied. The winning candidate is declared by the Returning Officer.

❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the Indian election process.

The MCC is a set of guidelines issued by the ECI that comes into force from the date elections are announced. It regulates the conduct of political parties, candidates, and the government to ensure a level playing field. Key rules include no policy announcements, no misuse of government machinery, and no personal attacks based on religion or caste.
NOTA (None of the Above) was introduced by the Supreme Court in 2013. It allows voters to reject all candidates without revealing their identity. However, even if NOTA gets the highest votes, the candidate with the most votes among the contestants still wins. NOTA votes are counted and reported but don't affect the election outcome directly.

An Electronic Voting Machine (EVM) is a standalone, battery-operated device made by BEL and ECIL. Key safety features: no internet connectivity, one-time programmable chips, unique ID for each machine, mandatory mock polls, and tamper-evident seals. The VVPAT (Voter Verified Paper Audit Trail) adds an additional layer of verification.
The ECI accepts 12 types of photo identity documents: EPIC (Voter ID), Aadhaar Card, Passport, Driving License, PAN Card, Smart Card issued by RGI, MNREGA Job Card, Health Insurance Smart Card, Bank/Post Office Passbook with photo, Service ID (Govt./PSU employees), MP/MLA/MLC Identity Card, and Pension document with photo.
